AI systems generate plausible language from patterns in data. They can organize information quickly, but they can also combine accurate facts with false conclusions, omit important qualifications, or present unsettled claims as established science.

Health information is especially vulnerable because context matters. A statement may be true for one population, dose, diagnosis, or outcome and misleading when generalized.

## A better way to use AI

Treat an AI answer as a starting point for questions, not as clinical authority. Ask what evidence supports the claim, whether the sources are peer reviewed, how many studies agree, whether the research applies to you, and what important exceptions were left out.

The first principle of critical thinking is not merely to distrust technology. It is to remain aware that the easiest person to fool is ourselves—particularly when an answer confirms what we already hoped was true.
