## My background

I am Jennifer Nickell, a registered dietitian with more than fifteen years of experience in patient care, teaching, nutrition, family systems, and human development. My education includes graduate work in nutrition and food science, family and consumer sciences, transformational leadership, and additional training in neurodevelopment and pediatrics.

That broad background changes the questions I ask. I am interested not only in *what* people eat, but in how they learned to relate to food, how their nervous systems respond to stress, what resources their environments provide, and whether the advice they have received is scientifically sound or commercially convenient.

## My approach

I do not treat nutrition as a test of discipline. Human behavior is constrained and enabled by biology, learning, time, money, relationships, culture, and the physical environment. Sustainable change requires more than being told what to do.

My role is to help you:

- understand what your body is doing;
- identify the beliefs and conditions shaping your choices;
- reduce fear, shame, and confusion;
- build practical supports that fit your real life; and
- develop a more stable, compassionate relationship with food and your body.

## Why education matters

You do not need to remember every pathway in human metabolism. But when one piece of science rings true—when it helps explain an experience you have had—it can strengthen the framework you use to make future decisions.

That is why my teaching goes deeper than a list of recommendations but stops short of unnecessary complexity. The goal is not memorization. The goal is understanding.
